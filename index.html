Guide

Your quick reference

When an urge hits

1
Notice it
Catch the precursor: hand moving to mouth, thumb scanning for rough edges. Awareness is half the battle.
2
Name the trigger
Stress? Boredom? Rough edge? Identifying it weakens the autopilot response.
3
Do the competing response
Pick one from the table below. Hold it for 30-60 seconds. The urge will pass.
4
Log it
Tap "Resisted" on the Log tab. Tracking reinforces the new pattern.

Coping techniques

Fist clench
Clench both fists firmly, press against thighs, hold 30s. Engages the same hand muscles and makes biting physically impossible. Pair with box breathing.
Box breathing
Breathe in 4s, hold 4s, out 4s, hold 4s. Addresses the underlying anxiety rather than just blocking the behaviour.
Fidget tool
Textured fidget ring, worry stone, or coin. Run your thumb across it. Research shows ~50% of people find this sufficient on its own.
Nail file
When you find a rough edge, file it smooth instead of biting. Removes the physical stimulus entirely.
Finger rub
Gently rub fingertips together or rub thumb across palm for 10 seconds. Simple, discreet, works anywhere.

Environment design

Make biting harder and alternatives easier:
Keep nails short
Less material to bite, fewer rough edges to trigger picking.
Moisturise cuticles
Hangnails are a major trigger. Cuticle oil pen, once or twice daily.
Seed your spaces
Fidget tool on desk, by the sofa, in your coat pocket. Grip strengthener next to keyboard. Nail file in wallet.

When you slip

A slip is not a reset. If you bite one nail, stop there. Log it, note the trigger, and move on. The goal is progress, not perfection. Your streak tracks clean days, but the real measure is your success rate over time.
